About

I am currently an assistant professor in Radboud University at the AI department and a visiting assistant professor at the Yale School of Management. My main research focus is on multiagent interaction, complex systems and reinforcment learning

Research

Complex Systems

Investigating the dynamics of interconnected systems and emergent collective behaviors. I explore how simple interaction rules give rise to sophisticated system-level phenomena, with applications to distributed intelligence and adaptive networks.

Chemical AI

Developing machine learning methods at the interface of chemistry and artificial intelligence. This work focuses on using AI to predict molecular properties, design novel compounds, and understand chemical reaction mechanisms.

Game Theoretical Scenarios Using Deep Learning

Applying deep reinforcement learning to game theoretic problems. I study strategic interactions in multi-agent environments, equilibrium concepts, and the emergence of cooperative and competitive behaviors in learned policies.

LLM and Multi-Agent Interactions

Exploring how large language models interact in multi-agent settings. This research examines communication protocols, coordination mechanisms, and emergent linguistic behaviors when multiple language models collaborate or compete.

Information For Prospective Students

It's so awesome you want to come and do a graduate degree with me! I would love to hear from. Unfortunately I get waaaaaaay too many emails these days. I I highly recommend reading this nifty little guide to writing faculty members

I can take students either through Radboud AI or the data science program, I welcome any and all students.

Albeit please consider a few things.

I highly recommend looking at the groups emphasis please take a look in the research tab. I love hearing bright new ideas and non-obvious insights

For M.Sc students:

  • Have you checked out the things I am scientifically interested, and working on ? Please do to see if we are aligned
  • I usually don't take students without meeting them and talking to them at least once.
  • You need to have a very high level of mathematical/analytical skills. Wonder what that? is look at some of my publications
  • Not a must but a great bonus if you took my course Applied Machine Learning
  • I highly encourage you to talk to my other students

For Ph.D students:

  • If you are interested in our work, and your mind is exploding with creative ideas, please contact us to explore possibilities
  • I am always happy to talk to any and all students on anything science related.

If you come from an interdisciplinary background that is a huge plus and should not discourage you from working with the group. I love interacting with people from all disciplines .

I usually don't take students without meeting them and working with them a bit to see the fit.

There are a lot of funding opportunities out there, I am more than happy to work with students to obtain them. Have your own funding? Fantastic! If not, please see the groups website to see if I have ways to fund you already

Also feel free to look at the FAQ page which I continually update.

Good luck with your search!!
