Teaching

Members of the CATALYST Lab are committed to education and mentorship across undergraduate, master's, and PhD levels. We teach courses that bridge theory and practice in AI, machine learning, and computational sciences.

Teaching Philosophy

Our teaching approach emphasizes:

  • Theory Meets Practice: We balance mathematical rigor with hands-on implementation, ensuring students understand both the "why" and the "how."
  • Research-Driven Learning: Course content reflects current research trends, exposing students to cutting-edge developments in the field.
  • Active Engagement: We encourage questions, discussions, and collaborative problem-solving to deepen understanding.
  • Real-World Applications: Projects and examples connect course material to practical challenges in industry and research.

Student Supervision

We actively supervise students at all levels:

  • PhD Students: Deep, long-term mentorship on cutting-edge research problems. Students are encouraged to pursue ambitious ideas and develop independent research skills.
  • Master's Students: Thesis supervision on focused research questions, often leading to publications and continued work as PhD students.
  • Bachelor's Students: Guided research projects that provide hands-on experience with real research methodologies and tools.
